Track 3: Strategies for Critical Mass

Continuous Solidarity: A Conversation about Youth Activism


Description

In 1969, Robert Neil Butler coined the term “ageism” to describe the widespread hostile prejudice faced by the elderly. However, ageism has now been altered to include the way mainstream society think of the youth. Asian American youth must now balance the challenges of youth while pursuing a track of advocacy. Many times, students may feel as though the spaces of activism are dominated by adults, and that their input is unnecessary. This workshop is aimed on giving youth activists/organizers the space to talk about their own experiences and strategies they have used to confront some of the tensions of operating within adult-dominated organizations and giving recommendations for other youth interested in carving their own activist spaces. Teaching about solidarity across age limits can be a way to educate people about activism and how it leads to current day issues.
