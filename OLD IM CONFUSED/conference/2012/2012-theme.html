Theme Statement

So maybe you've spent your entire life listening to Tiger Mom. Maybe you've always been the teacher's pet and did what everyone else told you to do. You obligingly got up every morning and dragged yourself to school. You lived and breathed facts about the English in America, the Irish in America, and the Italians in America. But no one ever said anything about Asians in America, so you assumed there was nothing to say.

Luckily for you, we're living in an exciting time as Asian Americans. Turn on the TV and you'll find Jeremy Lin tearing it up on the basketball court or Kal Penn on hit TV shows. Tune into the radio and you can dance to Far East Movement. Check the bestsellers list and you'll find the name Jhumpa Lahiri. Never before has our visibility been so far reaching. However, even if you can't ball like Lin, act like Penn, rap like Nish, or write like Lahiri you can still be heard. You don't have to be what they want you to be.


This year the New York City Asian American Student Conference (NYCAASC) would like to invite you to ask yourselves "Who are we as Asian Americans?" While often glossed over in history books and lumped together as a group, we are extremely diverse and have been an integral part in shaping American history. But don't just take our word for it. By attending NYCAASC and the conference's workshops, we hope to start a dialogue with you. We hope you'll gain a better understanding of who you are as an Asian American. More importantly, we hope you'll see yourself as an empowered individual.


So what are you waiting for? Register and start INKing your own story right now.

The New York City Asian American Student Conference 2007 - 2016.
