Critical Mass

Theme Statement

As Asian Americans, our culture is replete with diverse narratives that make us unique, not only amongst one another but in relation to other communities. Deeply ingrained within our consciousness is the knowledge of our collective and personal origins. However, when we situate our Asian American history within mainstream chronology, our stories are invisible: our roots are severed; accomplishments are disregarded; and icons of heritage are reappropriated. We are termed the “model minority,” yet its after-effects are anything but laudatory.

Thriving upon the achievements of our Asian American forebears, it is our turn to explore beyond the horizons in order to elevate our community to a new level of awareness and recognition. As such, we should actively seek to empower ourselves past the limitations placed before us. We have made our voices known in the spheres of politics, entertainment, social justice, and other pertinent civil intersections, but the battle is far from won. The veil of oppression and injustice hides behind various personas, and, as a group, we must undergo varying degrees of metamorphoses to stay ahead of the game.


This year, the theme of NYCAASC is “Change in Motion” and follows closely with the motifs of previous conferences. At the very first conference, NYCAASC wished to “Break the Silence” regarding the state of Asian America. As an inception, it unleashed dialogue that cast a critical eye on the junctures of race and identity. Afterwards, the conference pursued to “Redefine” what it truly means to be Asian American. This heralded discussions to crystallize the distinct perspectives that span within and beyond our community. Once discourse was in place, it was time to “ACT” (Activate, Challenge, Transform). Last year, the conference was a source with which community members could mobilize and organize change on behalf of the Asian American community.


Keeping in line with preceding themes, “Change in Motion” intends to propel the established momentum of furthering an Asian American presence. Forces that interact with the notion of being Asian American radiate from numerous origins, and deeper analyses of these influences require deconstruction that is tenacious yet mercurial. For example, one needs to understand the inherent role that the mainstream plays regarding the invisibility of Asian Americans, while at a moment’s notice, plan and enact methods to address such discrimination in conjunction with fostering multi-community solidarity. Knowledge of the interrelation of theory and practice provides us with the tools to evaluate and recreate a more fitting Asian American historical and mythic narrative. By discerning how individual and aggregate hierarchies and institutions manipulate particular outcomes, we, as an Asian American collective, can affect our own hopes and desires for transformation.

The New York City Asian American Student Conference 2007 - 2016.
